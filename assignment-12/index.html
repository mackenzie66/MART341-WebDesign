History

Known as the Backbone of the World to the Blackfeet Nation, the rugged landscape
of Glacier attracted the attention of the Great Northern Railway in the early 1890s.
Though the railroad was simply seeking a route to the West Coast, its president saw in
the rugged, alp-like landscape the potential for tourism.
